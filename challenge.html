Challenge Details

We plan to run a competition with our workshop. We will provide a high-resolution satellite image dataset (courtesy of DigitalGlobe) for a disaster area in a developing country. The challenge will be posed as learning the expected urban elements for each category as detailed below.
We will provide our own results for each category to establish a baseline. The live scores, the submission and evaluation of the results, and the dataset will be maintained in the website of the workshop. For each submission, the scripts will compare the mean average precision (MAP) on a hidden test dataset, which we will also make available after the workshop. The challengers will also be required to submit a short paper (4 pages) detailing their methodology, which can be later extended as a full paper for further publication.
The winner paper in each category will receive a cash prize sponsored by Facebook, DigitalGlobe, and other tentative sponsors. The top five submissions in each category will be chosen as finalists and will be invited to present their research in the poster session. We expect around 20 submissions for each category, around 60 submissions in total. The categories are:
• Road extraction: We expect the challengers to provide better results in terms of the accuracy of the current best road extraction method. This will be a binary segmentation problem to detect all roads in a given area. The evaluation will be based on completeness and connectedness of the roads.
• Building detection: We expect the challengers to provide better results in terms of the accuracy of the current best building detection method. This will be a binary segmentation problem to detect all buildings in a given area.
• Land cover classification: We expect the challengers to provide better results in terms of the accuracy of the current best land cover classification method. This will be a multi-class segmentation problem to detect all given land types in a given area. The land types will be man-made, forest, waterbody, agricultural, grass, barren, cloud, and unknown.
